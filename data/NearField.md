## Motivation
As Integrated Sensing and Communication (ISAC)—a leading 6G candidate technology actively discussed in 3GPP—matures, large antenna arrays (XL-MIMO), high-frequency bands (mmWave/THz and the upper-mid band), and the close placement of users and targets are pushing communication and sensing into the **near-field** regime. Owing to spherical-wave propagation, the near-field provides an additional **range-dimension degree of freedom**, enabling joint range–angle sensing and improved interference control. Real systems, however, are hybrid environments where far-field and near-field coexist, and commercial-grade demonstrations of near-field sensing/ISAC do not yet exist, remaining largely at the theory and simulator stage. Our laboratory (KAIST) has particular strength in **near-field sensing of wide-area extended targets** and ISAC beamformer design built upon it, and aims to advance step by step from precise near-field channel modeling to AI-based monostatic and multistatic distributed near-field ISAC.

## Core Objectives
**Final goal —** Secure **near-field precision-sensing core technologies** for radio-based 6G ISAC by exploiting spherical-wave propagation, advancing from monostatic toward an **AI-based multistatic, distributed near-field ISAC network**
- Precise near-field channel modeling and characterization of the theoretical limits of sensing performance
- 5G-A-compatible monostatic near-field high-precision sensing/beam focusing and near-/far-field seamless localization
- AI-based low-complexity near-field sensing and intelligent ISAC resource allocation
- Multistatic distributed near-field sensing coverage/resolution extension and AI-fused network optimization

## Core Technologies (by year)
- **Year 1 (2026) — Near-field propagation modeling & sensing-limit analysis:** establishment of a precise near-field channel model extending 3GPP TR 38.901 (reflecting effective-aperture effects of large arrays and polarization-mismatch loss), and analysis of the theoretical lower bounds of range/angle estimation for 5G-A-based near-field sensing
- **Year 2 (2027) — 5G-A-compatible monostatic near-field high-precision sensing/beam focusing:** monostatic near-field ISAC beam focusing that maximizes CRB/detection accuracy while meeting Rel-19 low-latency/high-reliability requirements, and hybrid seamless localization combining far-field (time-of-flight) and near-field (spherical-wave phase) information
- **Year 3 (2028) — AI-based monostatic near-field sensing enhancement:** low-complexity real-time target sensing via neural networks that learn polar-domain sparsity of the near-field, and intelligent ISAC resource allocation that dynamically determines the optimal operating point between data rate and sensing accuracy based on the 5G-A AI/ML air interface
- **Year 4 (2029) — Multistatic near-field sensing & coverage extension:** multistatic network configuration using distributed Radio Units to extend the effective near-field region, and multistatic near-field sensing that combines distinct spherical-wave phase information from distributed nodes to improve spatial resolution/degrees of freedom, with theoretical estimation-accuracy bound analysis
- **Year 5 (2030) — AI-based multistatic information fusion & network optimization:** high-dimensional fusion sensing in which an AI network combines observations from distributed nodes to reduce estimation error, and AI-native distributed-ISAC sensing-network optimization that jointly controls beam focusing and resources across multiple RUs
