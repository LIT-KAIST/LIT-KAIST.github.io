## Motivation
Future mobility services such as autonomous driving and UAM require both **cm-level ultra-precise positioning** and **1 Tbps-class ultra-high-capacity communication** *simultaneously*. However, conventional approaches that operate communication and sensing as separate systems are limited in terms of resource efficiency and latency. This calls for an **I²SAC convergence system** that performs communication and sensing at the same time using multiple antennas and a single waveform, while intelligently exploiting the large-scale sensing data distributed across the network. Furthermore, intelligent techniques such as semantic communication, multimodal AI, and transfer/meta learning are integrated to secure low latency and adaptability to environmental changes.

## Core Objectives
**Final goal —** Research on an **ultra-precise, ultra-high-capacity I²SAC system** that intelligently utilizes diverse communication and sensing resources
1. **Ultra-precise sensing** technology to achieve cm-level positioning error
2. **Communication** technology to support ultra-high-capacity traffic of 1 Tbps or more
3. **Integrated communication–sensing** technology that performs communication and sensing simultaneously using multiple antennas and a single waveform
4. **Intelligent communication–sensing cooperation** technology for dense distributed networks

## Core Technologies (by year)
- **Year 1 — System modeling & analysis:** I²SAC communication system modeling, KPI definition and performance target derivation, sensing-accuracy vs. communication data-rate trade-off analysis
- **Year 2 — Individual sensing & communication technologies:** waveforms and antenna structures for ultra-precise sensing, intelligent precise positioning based on sensing data, joint resource allocation and beamforming via unsupervised learning, position prediction for high-mobility nodes, position-based blind channel estimation, Delay-Doppler channel modeling
- **Year 3 — Communication–sensing convergence:** integrated MIMO beamforming, interference-controlled integrated waveform, single-waveform beamforming jointly optimizing sensing error and spectral efficiency, Delay-Doppler mobility techniques, low-latency (FBL-code-based) ISAC transmission/reception
- **Year 4 — Dense networks:** multi-base-station task/resource allocation optimization, throughput maximization using position information, 3D cooperation via UAV/IRS reinforcement learning, multiple-access integrated MIMO beamforming, multimodal-learning channel estimation *(in collaboration with Subtask 2)*
- **Year 5 — Adaptive I²SAC:** analysis of non-ideal characteristics of low-power hardware and low-power MIMO/hybrid beamforming, environment-adaptive sensing based on transfer/meta learning, reinforcement-learning-based placement optimization of multiple UAVs/IRSs
- **Year 6 — Security & semantic:** I²SAC secure communication system based on physical-layer security, semantic communication techniques for high-capacity traffic reduction *(in collaboration with Subtask 3)*
- **Year 7 — Integrated simulation:** I²SAC integrated network simulator design, performance verification, and system review
- **Year 8 — Feasibility verification & final evaluation *(in collaboration with Subtask 4)*:** implementation of an integrated simulator under future-mobility scenarios — simultaneous achievement of miniature-vehicle positioning error and spectral efficiency, latency reduction for FBL-based intersection control, Blind Kalman channel-estimation evaluation, energy efficiency (EE), and secrecy-rate SNR-gain measurement
